### **1. Introduction: What are Options?**

An option is a financial contract that gives the buyer the **right, but not the obligation**, to buy or sell an underlying asset (e.g., stock, ETF, index) at a specified price (the **strike price**) on or before a certain date (the **expiration date**).

*   **Put Option:** Gives the holder the right to **sell** the underlying asset. Typically used for bearish or neutral strategies.
*   **Key Participants:**
    *   **Buyer (Holder):** Pays a premium for the right. Risk is limited to the premium paid.
    *   **Seller (Writer):** Receives the premium but takes on the obligation to buy or sell if assigned. Risk can be substantial or unlimited.
